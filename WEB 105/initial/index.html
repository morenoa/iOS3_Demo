The Schafberg (literally "Mount the Sheep") is a mountain in the Alps culminating at 1782 m above sea level and located in the mountains of the Salzkammergut ( Land of Salzburg ) in Austria , representative of the Northern Limestone Alps .

The summit of Schafberg is crowned by Schafbergspitze Hotel, built in 1862 by Wolfgang Grommer few meters from the vertiginous peak of the north face, making it the first hotel establishment erected atop a mountain in history Austrian tourism. The hotel has double rooms with shower, toilet and balcony to the south side.
