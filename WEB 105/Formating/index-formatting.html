The Afghan Campaign

The Summary

This is a soldier story. where 2,300 years he marches with an unbeaten army of the west to invaded the land of a fierce eastern enemy.

In this noval, an ancient warfare returns to the re-creation of Alexander the Great’s invasion of the Afghan kingdoms in 330 b.c. Steven Pressfield brings to life the confrontation between an invading western army and eastern warriors. Who are determined at all costs to defend their homeland. Narrated by an infantryman in Alexander’s army, The Afghan Campaign explores the challenges, both military and moral, that Alexander and his soldiers face as they embark on a new type of war and are forced to adapt to the and ruthless foe that employs terror and insurgent tactics. The Afghan Campaign brings out Pressfield’s profound hopes and desperation of a men in battle and the historical realities that continue to influence our world.

The Plot

Young Matthias from Macedonia follows his two older brothers example and enrolls in Alexander’s cavalry together with his close friend Lucas. He takes part of his first battle and gets shocked by the guerilla methods, recruits women and children to fight for their freedom. While marching, Matthias meets Shinar, an Afghan woman who, having abandoned her own people, offers her services as carrier of the Greek army’s supplies. Nanguali is the barbarian warrior’s code; its three elements are: honour, revenge and hospitality. Their women’s honour, if blackened, could be redeemed only by death. Matthias stands up to Baz, Shinar’s brother, but fails to reach a compromise and is deceived by Baz, who in the end kills his sister and her baby.At the end of the story, Matthias is left with nothing – he has lost his family, friends, health and hope. Instead of returning home as initially planned and having nothing to lose, he decides to follow the Greek army in its way to India.

About the Author

Steven Pressfield is the author and he was born in Port of Spain, Trinidad, in 1943. Since graduating from Duke University in 1965, he has been a U.S. Marine, an advertising copywriter, schoolteacher, tractor-trailer driver, bartender, oilfield roustabout, attendant in a mental hospital and screenwriter. It is one thing to study war, and another to live the warrior's life.
